Инновационная система образования в HTA

Minerva Baccalaureate (MBacc) is an advanced high school program that prepares students for even the most demanding university education.

The program integrates core subjects - science, mathematics, social studies, and language arts into an interdisciplinary curriculum with shared learning outcomes developed across courses and contexts.

Each course has two parts. Students build foundational knowledge through guided self-study, then apply it in small, instructor-led seminars where they analyze, discuss, and solve complex problems.

There are no lectures. Class time is dedicated to active learning - practicing skills, working with ideas, and applying knowledge in new situations.

Student progress is assessed through clearly defined learning outcomes, with detailed feedback that supports continuous development.

In the fourth optional year, students take university-level courses and may earn transferable college credits.
